FAQ

Quick Answers

Everything you need to know before you scan.

How accurate is the heart risk prediction?

85%+ accuracy on the UCI Cleveland Heart Disease test set, verified by 5-fold cross-validation. Source: UCI Heart Disease Dataset and Detrano et al. (1989).

Can this replace my doctor?

No — and it's designed not to. This is a first-step screening tool. A High-risk result means you should book a cardiologist appointment. A Low-risk result is reassuring, but not a guarantee. Your doctor always has the final word.

What if I don't know some of the values?

All inputs have a "?" tooltip explaining what each field means. For values you don't know (like ST depression or blocked arteries), we recommend entering 0 — which reflects a normal/unknown baseline. The scan will still give you a useful directional result.

Is my health data stored?

No. Your inputs are sent to the prediction API, a result is returned, and everything is discarded. We don't ask for your name, email, or any identifying information to use the scanner.
