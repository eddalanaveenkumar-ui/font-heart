FAQ

Quick Answers

How accurate is the heart risk prediction?

85%+ accuracy on the UCI Cleveland test set, verified by 5-fold cross-validation. Source: UCI Heart Disease Dataset and Detrano et al. (1989).

Can this replace my doctor?

No. This is a first-step screening tool. A High-risk result means you should book a cardiologist. A Low-risk result is reassuring but not a guarantee. Always consult a doctor for diagnosis and treatment.

Is my health data safe?

Yes. Your inputs are used only for the instant prediction call. Nothing is stored, logged, or shared. We don't even ask for your name or email to use the scanner.

What does a High-risk result mean?

It means the AI detected a pattern consistent with higher cardiovascular risk. It does NOT mean you will have a heart attack. It means you should discuss these results with a cardiologist as soon as possible — ideally within 1–2 weeks.
